Your Final Exam timetable for Term 2 will be visible on myUNSW on Monday 17 July.

The final timetable will display the location, date, and time of your exams and list authorised materials.

Before going to your exams, you must be aware of your student responsibilities under the UNSW Exam Rules and Assessment Policy.

Updates to Exam delivery

With the transition back to in-person classes and activity on campus, there will be some changes in the way exams are delivered, including the return of face-to-face exams and the continued use of our online exam platform, Inspera.

Check the updates to exam delivery page to understand these changes.

International students are expected to be onshore for the compulsory study weeks, including the exam period.

Multi-Factor Authentication

Multi-Factor Authentication is a requirement at UNSW for all students and staff. MFA provides an additional layer of security to help protect the University and your account from unauthorised access. To ensure you can access your UNSW accounts during your exam, you must have your authenticator smartphone fully charged and available on the day of your in-person or online exam.

Library Access

The UNSW Main Library, Law Library and Paddington Library are all available to be used as study spaces, including some bookable individual and group spaces. Plus, if you need to borrow a laptop to sit your on-campus, in-person exam, you can check availability at the library.

Special Consideration

With online delivery of exams, it's especially important to read up on the Special Consideration process so that you are prepared for any unexpected issues.

Timetable Clash

If you have an ‘all day’ exam that coincides with another assessment, this is not considered a clash, as you will have all day to complete the exam at a time that suits you.

Examination adjustments

Equitable Learning Plans (ELP)

If you have an ELP that includes exam adjustments, please confirm these with your course convenor or school, if you have not already done so.

The cut-off date for any changes to the adjustments in your ELP is Wednesday 2 August.

Please contact Equitable Learning Services (ELS) if you have questions.

 

Religious obligations

If your exam conflicts with your religious obligations, please complete a Special Consideration application, and make sure you attach supporting documentation, by Wednesday 2 August.

Once your application is assessed, if approved, your timetable on myUNSW will be updated with any alternative arrangements.

Supplementary exams

If you are approved for a supplementary exam, your myUNSW timetable will be updated no later than Friday 1 September.

The supplementary exam period for Term 2 will be Monday 4 - Friday 8 September 2023.

Please regularly check your UNSW email and check the Supplementary Exam page for more information.
